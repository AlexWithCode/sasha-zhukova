What Was The Hardest Decision I Made?

When I was invited to a small transport company, where there was a little (no) administrative chaos, I was given carte blanche in my hands as the lead clerk to put things in order.

I had one employee who was subordinated to working in my team and who was the only responsible person for all administrative issues in this company. The result of my analysis of problems in the administrative sector was that my subordinate employee was not competent and it is she who makes a lot of stable mistakes.

I began to teach my colleague, transferring to her my knowledge of how the document flow should be managed. Thanks to her deep knowledge of the internal processes of the company, we modified the registers of documents and built the structure of document flow between departments.

But I was faced with the most difficult psychotype in all my work experience. After a while, my colleague began to make the same mistakes with the same frequency... because she just didn't want to "change habits." Due to my experience as an HR manager, I had no choice but to advise the CEO to change the specialist whom I could select.

Moreover, I was allowed to place two more employees in addition to the existing position. So I fired one employee but got three others in return, and we had a great team!
